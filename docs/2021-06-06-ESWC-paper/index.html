Problems with large inconsistent knowledge graphs

  • Inconsistent knowledge graphs block reasoning
  • Justifications can occur frequently
  • Justifications are domain dependent
  • Scalability issues with justifications

It is simple to find an inconsistent knowledge graph, but not simple to find all justifications

Motivation for this paper

We want to develop tooling that can:

  • Allows us to find mistakes in knowledge graphs
  • Allows us to fix the mistakes in knowledge graphs
  • Allows us to compare knowledge graphs
  • Allows us to build benchmarking systems

What is the use anti-patterns?

  • Scalability issues with justifications
  • Frequencies of justifications
  • Anti-patterns are domain independent

What is an justification?

Contradicting statements demonstrating the unsatisfiable class CheesyVegetableTopping

What is an anti-pattern?

Contradicting anti-pattern containing the essence of the justification

Part I: Research Questions

Research Question 1

Can we define a more general explanation for contradictions, that categorises the most common mistakes in a KG, independently from its domain?

Research Question 2

Can we retrieve these generalised explanations from any KG, independently from its size?

Research Question 3

How can these generalised explanations help analysing and comparing certain characteristics between the most commonly used KGs in the Web?

Part II: Formalization

Research Question 1

Definition 1 (Anti-Pattern) 


              (P) is an anti-pattern of a Knowledge Graph(G)

              if there is a substitution μ(P) s.t. μ(P)⊆G,
              and μ(P) is minimally inconsistent w.r.t some entailment relation (E),

              i.e. @P′ s.t. P′⊂P and μ(P′) is inconsistent.

Part VI: Conclusions

Contributions

  • (Q1) We introduced anti-patterns that match inconsistent subgraphs in a KG
  • (Q2) A tool that extracts a large number of anti-patterns from any KG
  • (Q3) A Catalog of anti-patterns that can help locate these contradictions in other KGs, via SPARQL

Future work

  • Applying this tool to also include additional datasets
  • exploiting the computed transitive closure in the LOD-a-lot, detecting additional anti-patterns
  • Analysing the origins of anti-patterns, analysing the detected justifications
